Description of Division II Concentration

My focuses in Division II will be on computer science, primarily at this time in programming, and syntax. Potentially, I will be studying computational linguistics.

I plan to spend this coming summer in Trier, attempting to bring my spoken German closer to fluency. From there, I am taking all of the core classes of a computer science degree at Amherst (skipping Intro to Comp Sci II). I plan to take more syntax courses, some computational linguistics courses, and assorted programming-oriented courses. This should give me a good base for pursuing a Div III in computer science, though I'm currently not sure where I will take my Div III

Multiple Cultural Perspectives

I believe that learning German has and will continue to prove vital to examining the way in which I think - languages are wonderful opportunities to look at other cultures from a very foundational level.

I will also continue to look out for classes that will help me continue to explore multi-cultural issues that I studied as part of Division I.

Further, I am a signer and part of the re-founding effort for an ambitious identity-based student group on campus, GRN (gender-identity resource network), formerly TSA (trans student alliance). We aim to be a framework and resource hub which can provide services of all sorts to trans students on campus, ranging from event and meeting organization help, to networking, to a hampedia page with useful links, to a combination-locked box with clothes for trans students.

This will be an incredible resource for trans students on campus, and I intend to write about it in my retrospective essay.
